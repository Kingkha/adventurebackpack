Essential Preparation for Your Thrilling Flight

Before you embark on your paragliding adventure, a little preparation goes a long way. First, dress comfortably in layers, as it can get cooler at altitude, even on a sunny day. Closed-toe shoes, preferably sneakers or hiking boots, are essential for takeoff and landing. Avoid sandals or heels. Most importantly, ensure you get a good night’s sleep and eat a light meal beforehand; an empty stomach isn’t ideal for adrenaline-pumping activities! Operators will provide all necessary safety gear, including helmets and harnesses, and give a thorough briefing. Don’t forget your camera, but make sure it’s securely attached! What to Expect on Your Paragliding Day

On the day of your Gyeongju paragliding flight, you’ll typically meet at a designated spot, often in the city center, before being transported to the launch site high in the mountains. Once there, your pilot will explain the process, including the short running take-off you’ll do together. The actual flight time usually ranges from 10 to 20 minutes, depending on wind conditions, but every second feels like an eternity of pure bliss. You’ll float effortlessly, enjoying incredible panoramic vistas. Landing is typically smooth, often in an open field, with your pilot guiding the descent. Most companies offer optional photo and video packages, which I highly recommend – it’s a moment you’ll want to relive! Capture all the amazing Gyeongju highlights from above.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is Gyeongju paragliding safe for beginners?

A: Yes, Gyeongju paragliding is very safe for beginners. Most operations offer tandem flights where you’re securely harnessed with a certified, experienced pilot. They handle all the flying, allowing you to relax and enjoy the breathtaking views with minimal effort or prior training.

Q: What is the best time of year to go paragliding in Gyeongju?

A: The spring (April-May) and autumn (September-October) months are generally considered the best for paragliding in Gyeongju. During these seasons, the weather is milder, visibility is excellent, and wind conditions are often ideal for a smooth and enjoyable flight experience over the scenic landscapes.

Q: How much does paragliding typically cost in Gyeongju in 2025?

A: In 2025, Gyeongju paragliding costs for a tandem flight typically range from ₩100,000 to ₩150,000 KRW (approximately $75-$115 USD), depending on the operator and any included photo/video packages. Prices can fluctuate slightly, so it’s always best to check with individual companies for their current rates.
