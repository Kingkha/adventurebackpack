Savannah Itinerary: Your Ultimate 3-Day Guide

Savannah, Georgia, is steeped in history and rich Southern charm, making it a captivating destination for travelers. This meticulously crafted Savannah itinerary takes you through beautifully preserved historic sites, lush city squares, and mouthwatering cuisine, ensuring you experience the cultural essence of this enchanting city. From walking tours and ghost stories to riverfront views, discover why Savannah is known as one of America’s most picturesque cities.

Key Takeaways

  • Day 1: Visit Forsyth Park, stroll through the Historic District, explore the Mercer-Williams House, and enjoy an evening ghost tour.
  • Day 2: Taste local favorites at the City Market, dine at traditional Southern eateries, and indulge in a riverboat cruise for lunch.
  • Day 3: Discover the SCAD Museum of Art, wander through Bonaventure Cemetery, and take a relaxing walk along the Savannah Riverfront.

Day 1: Uncover Historic Savannah

Welcome to your first day in Savannah, a city rich in history and Southern charm. To kickstart your Savannah itinerary, begin by immersing yourself in the historic ambiance of this stunning city.

9am: Forsyth Park

Your adventure begins at Forsyth Park, the crown jewel of Savannah. Spanning 30 acres, this park is known for its iconic fountain that dates back to 1858. Here, you can take a leisurely stroll along the walking paths, enjoy the vibrant flowerbeds, or relax under the shade of ancient oak trees draped in Spanish moss.

Pro Tip: Visit early in the morning to enjoy the tranquility before the crowds arrive, and don’t forget your camera to capture memorable moments!

11am: Historic District

After exploring Forsyth Park, make your way to the Historic District. This area is a treasure trove of preserved architecture, where you can admire stunning antebellum homes and cobblestone streets. Consider joining a guided walking tour to dive deeper into the fascinating history of Savannah’s early settlers and the city’s role in the Civil War.

As you wander through the district, be sure to pass by the Savannah City Hall, famous for its striking golden dome. It’s a perfect photo-op spot!

1pm: Mercer-Williams House

Next, head to the Mercer-Williams House, an architectural masterpiece and the site of the infamous murder case depicted in Midnight in the Garden of Good and Evil. Take a guided tour to learn about the house’s history, the intriguing stories behind its former residents, and the decorative arts collection housed within.

Allow yourself to bask in the ornate details, from the lush gardens to the exquisite interiors, which provide a glimpse into Savannah’s opulent past.

3pm: Charlotte’s Cafe for Lunch

After your visit, break for lunch at Charlotte’s Cafe, an eatery known for serving wholesome Southern fare. Their fried green tomatoes and shrimp and grits are local favorites that you simply cannot miss!

5pm: Evening Ghost Tour

As dusk falls, prepare for an exhilarating ghost tour through Savannah’s haunted streets. Join an expert guide who will tell you chilling tales of the city’s haunted history, from vengeful spirits to the intriguing stories of Savannah’s past residents. This adventure adds an exciting twist to your day, showcasing the deep-rooted tales of the South.

“Savannah is, without a doubt, one of the most haunted cities in America.”

8pm: Dinner at The Grey

Complete your day with dinner at The Grey, a renowned restaurant located in a restored 1938 Greyhound bus terminal. Here, you’ll savor contemporary Southern cuisine crafted by chef Mashama Bailey, whose menu draws on her family’s legacy and the culinary traditions of Savannah.

Make sure to sample their signature dishes, which are prepared using fresh, local ingredients that reflect the essence of the Lowcountry.

Conclusion

Your first day in Savannah offers a rich mix of history and culture, setting the stage for exciting Experiences to come. With a well-rounded Savannah itinerary, you’ll find connections to the city’s storied past while enjoying its vibrant present.

Day 2: Experience Savannah’s Culinary Delights

Savannah is a culinary paradise that offers an array of mouthwatering dishes and delightful local flavors. Starting your day at the City Market is the perfect way to dive into the vibrant food Culture of this historic city. The market, filled with art galleries, shops, and eateries, has something for everyone and serves as a great spot to savor some local favorites.

Morning: Breakfast at the City Market

Kick off your culinary adventure with a delicious breakfast at one of the charming cafes in City Market. Try out the classic Biscuits and Gravy or a refreshing Southern Pecan Waffle paired with locally sourced syrup. There’s something undeniably comforting about a warm, homemade breakfast that sets the mood for the day ahead.

“Savannah’s culinary scene is a delightful blend of tradition and innovation, promising flavors that will leave you wanting more.”

Mid-Morning: Explore Local Treats

After breakfast, spend some time exploring the local shops and vendors. The market often features artisanal products, including handmade chocolates, jams, and spices, perfect for picking up souvenirs or gifts. Don’t forget to try a Praline, a classic Southern confection that melts in your mouth!

Lunch: Dine at Traditional Southern Eateries

For lunch, indulge in a meal at one of Savannah’s traditional eateries, such as Mrs. Wilkes’ Dining Room or The Grey. Both spots focus on authentic Southern cuisine. Try the fried chicken, collard greens, or shrimp and grits—each dish a true celebration of Southern flavors.

Afternoon: Relax with a Riverboat Cruise

What better way to enjoy your time in Savannah than indulging in a delightful riverboat cruise while savoring a meal? Book a lunch cruise along the Savannah River to enjoy views of the historic waterfront. Many cruises offer buffet-style dining featuring Southern cuisine and drinks while you soak in the stunning scenery.

Evening: Dessert and Nightlife

After spending the day tantalizing your taste buds, it’s time to explore Savannah’s Nightlife. Start by treating yourself to dessert at Leopold’s Ice Cream, where you can indulge in rich, homemade ice cream in a variety of delectable flavors. For an authentic Southern experience, don’t miss the River Street area, lined with lively bars and live music venues.

“Savannah is as much about the shared experience of its food as it is about the flavors. Gather around the table, share stories, and make memories.”

Your culinary day in Savannah is not just about eating; it’s about embracing the city’s soul through its flavors and traditions. Make sure you savor each moment and meal, turning your Savannah itinerary into a delicious adventure!

Day 3: Explore Cultural Gems and Nature

On Day 3 of your Savannah itinerary, immerse yourself in the rich culture and natural beauty that this charming city has to offer. From art to history and tranquil parks, today’s Adventures will showcase the best of Savannah’s cultural gems and stunning landscapes.

Morning: Visit the SCAD Museum of Art

Start your day with an inspiring visit to the SCAD Museum of Art, a key highlight on any Savannah itinerary. Located in the historic district, this museum is dedicated to contemporary art and features a diverse collection from renowned and emerging artists. The Savannah College of Art and Design (SCAD) utilizes the museum as a creative hub, often showcasing student and faculty work along with exhibitions from impressive visiting artists.

As you wander through the museum’s galleries, take time to appreciate the thought-provoking installations and rotating exhibits. The museum’s modern architecture beautifully contrasts with the classic Savannah buildings outside, making it a photogenic stop on your journey.

Midday: Bonaventure Cemetery

After exploring the museum, head over to the Bonaventure Cemetery, one of Savannah’s most famous Landmarks. This historic cemetery is not only a resting place for some of Savannah’s most notable residents but also a serene location featuring beautiful oak trees draped with Spanish moss and stunning monuments.

A stroll through the paths of Bonaventure takes you through picturesque landscapes and artistic gravestones, some dating back to the 18th century. As you walk, you’ll find unique sculptures and serene areas that provide perfect spots for reflection and photography. Don’t forget to bring a camera!

Afternoon: Stroll Along the Savannah Riverfront

After a contemplative morning at the cemetery, treat yourself to a leisurely afternoon walk along the Savannah Riverfront. This area is filled with shops, cafes, and beautiful views of the river. Enjoy the lively atmosphere as street performers and vendors bring energy to the surroundings.

For lunch, consider dining at one of the riverfront restaurants that offer delicious Southern cuisine and stunning riverside views. dishes featuring fresh seafood and traditional dishes like shrimp and grits are a must-try.

Pro Tip: Try to visit the riverfront during sunset for a truly breathtaking view as the sky transforms into colors of orange and pink—perfect for a memorable photo opportunity.

Evening: Relax at Forsyth Park

As the day winds down, head to Forsyth Park, an iconic location in Savannah’s historic district. This expansive park is known for its picturesque fountain, walking paths, and vibrant gardens. It’s a perfect place to unwind after a day packed with exploration.

Consider packing a light picnic to enjoy on the grass or simply stroll through the park as the evening light softens the surroundings. On some evenings, you might even find live music or local Events adding to the park’s lively ambiance, making it an enjoyable way to conclude your day.

Day 3 of your Savannah itinerary is all about exploring the blend of Culture and natural beauty that defines this charming city. With art that inspires, nature that refreshes, and food that delights, you’ll leave with unforgettable memories and a deeper appreciation for Savannah.

Best Time to Visit: Seasonal Highlights for Your Savannah Itinerary

When planning your Savannah itinerary, understanding the best times to visit is crucial for an unforgettable experience. Savannah, Georgia, offers unique seasonal Highlights that cater to every traveler’s preferences. Notably, the city shines with beauty and activity throughout the year—each season presenting its own distinctive charm.

Spring (March to May) is arguably the most popular time to visit Savannah. As the weather warms up, blooming azaleas and magnolias paint the city in vibrant colors. The annual Savannah Tour of Homes and Gardens typically takes place in April, allowing visitors to explore stunning historic homes and their gardens. Additionally, Forsyth Park becomes a lively hub where locals and tourists alike gather to enjoy outdoor festivities. The pleasant temperatures, averaging around 70°F (21°C), make for ideal conditions to stroll through the Historic District or immerse yourself in the cultural displays in Chippewa Square.

Pro Tip: Book your accommodations well in advance if you’re planning to visit during this peak season, as hotels fill up quickly!

As summer roll in from June through August, temperatures soar, often exceeding 90°F (32°C). However, summer showcases Savannah’s lively festival scene, such as the Savannah Music Festival and Independence Day celebrations. While exploring the Historic District, try the traditional Southern drink, sweet tea, to cool off. Early mornings or late evenings are the best times for leisurely strolls. Consequently, while summer can be humid, it presents opportunities for vibrant outdoor gatherings and culinary events that you wouldn’t want to miss.

Autumn (September to November) offers a refreshing reprieve from the summer heat. With temperatures dropping to a comfortable 70-80°F (21-27°C), the season becomes ideal for enjoying Savannah’s outdoor spaces. Fall is a culturally rich time, with festivals like the Savannah Film Festival in late October and the Oktoberfest Celebration that showcase both local art and cuisine. During this time, you can also take advantage of seasonal offerings, such as pumpkin patches and local craft fairs.

Finally, winter (December to February) envelops Savannah in a quieter charm. While temperatures can dip to the mid-40s °F (7°C), the holiday festivities bring a festive ambiance to the city. Enjoy the beautiful Savannah Christmas Lights as Historic Savannah is adorned with twinkling lights and decorations. Though winter may not be the typical tourist season, it allows for a more relaxed exploration of the city’s attractions. If you’re lucky, you might catch a glimpse of the Savannah Riverboat Christmas Dinner Cruise, offering a warm and delightful dining experience on the river.

Season Average Temperature (°F) Highlights
Spring 70°F Azalea blooms, Savannah Tour of Homes and Gardens
Summer 90°F Savannah Music Festival, Independence Day celebrations
Autumn 70-80°F Savannah Film Festival, Oktoberfest Celebration
Winter Mid-40°F Savannah Christmas Lights, Riverboat Christmas Dinner Cruise

In conclusion, selecting the best time to visit Savannah is essential for maximizing your adventure. Consider both weather patterns and cultural Events when crafting your Savannah itinerary. Whichever season you choose, you’ll find that Savannah’s rich history, vibrant life, and natural beauty are waiting to envelop you.

Travel Tips for Your Savannah Itinerary: Getting Around and Local Fare

When crafting a Savannah itinerary, understanding how to navigate the city and its culinary scene is crucial. Savannah’s charm lies not only in its historic architecture but also in the delightful local fare and the ease of getting around. Here are essential tips and insights to enhance your visit.

Getting Around Savannah

Savannah is a walkable city, making it easy for travelers to explore its beauty on foot. However, here are additional transportation options that you might find useful:

  • Walking: The Historic District is compact, filled with cobblestone streets and picturesque parks. It’s best explored by foot, allowing you to appreciate the stunning architecture and lush greens.
  • Trolley Tours: For a fun and informative experience, consider taking a trolley tour. Trolleys offer hop-on/hop-off services, allowing you to visit key attractions at your own pace.
  • Ride-Sharing Services: Services like Uber and Lyft are readily available if you need to skip destinations that are farther apart.
  • Bicycles: Rent a bike through local services for a breezy ride around Forsyth Park and along the scenic riverfront.
