Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is Vigan safe for solo female travelers?

A: Yes, Vigan is generally considered very safe for solo female travelers. Locals are friendly and helpful. Always take standard precautions: be aware of your surroundings, especially at night, and keep your valuables secure. Staying in reputable hostels with good security features also adds peace of mind.

Q: What’s the best way to get around Vigan as a solo adventurer?

A: The heritage zone is best explored on foot or via kalesa (horse-drawn carriage). For areas slightly further out or for outdoor adventures, renting a bicycle is highly recommended and budget-friendly. Tricycles are also readily available for short distances, and they offer a convenient way to reach trailheads or other activity points.

Q: Are there many budget-friendly food options for solo travelers in Vigan?

A: Absolutely! Vigan is a food lover’s paradise, even on a budget. Street food stalls, particularly around the public market, offer delicious and affordable options like Vigan longganisa, empanadas, and sinanglao. Many local eateries serve hearty meals at very reasonable prices, ensuring you can fuel your adventures without breaking the bank.
