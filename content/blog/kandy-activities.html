Kandy Activities

Kandy is a picturesque city in Sri Lanka known for its rich cultural heritage and stunning landscapes. As the country’s last royal capital, it offers a unique blend of historical significance and natural beauty. In this article, we will guide you through 10 must-do activities in Kandy that will make your visit unforgettable. From exploring iconic temples to immersing yourself in local traditions and cuisine, these Kandy activities will provide you with an authentic taste of Sri Lankan culture and hospitality. Get ready to discover the heart of Kandy!

1. Visit the Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic: A Spiritual Journey

The Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic in Kandy is not just a religious site, it is a profound experience for every visitor. This temple houses a relic believed to be a tooth of the Buddha himself, making it an essential pilgrimage site for Buddhists. As you enter, you will feel a serene atmosphere enveloped by devotion and spirituality.

Moreover, you can participate in the daily rituals and witness the evening ceremony, which is truly enchanting. Guided tours are available to lead you through the temple’s storied history and architectural beauty. In addition to the relic, admire the ornate decorations and the historical artifacts that narrate the rich cultural heritage of Sri Lanka.

2. Explore the Royal Botanical Gardens: A Natural Paradise

If you seek tranquility amidst nature, you must explore the Royal Botanical Gardens in Kandy. Spanning over 147 acres, this lush paradise is home to thousands of plant species, including exotic orchids and towering palm trees. Additionally, the gardens are ideal for leisurely strolls or picnics with stunning views of the surrounding hills.

While wandering through, keep an eye out for the resident monkey families and other wildlife. Transitioning through different sections of the garden will reveal various ecosystems, each unique in its charm. Simply put, visiting the Royal Botanical Gardens offers a refreshing escape from the city’s hustle and bustle.

3. Discover Kandy Lake: A Tranquil Escape in the City

Kandy Lake is another jewel nestled in the heart of the city, creating a serene atmosphere perfect for relaxation. Surrounded by misty hills and lush greenery, the lake is ideal for a peaceful walk, especially during sunset. The picturesque scenery invites you to take memorable photographs as you enjoy a tranquil escape.

Additionally, you may rent a boat to paddle across the lake, providing a different perspective on the area’s natural beauty. As you explore the lakeside paths, be sure to watch for the rich birdlife that inhabits the area. Overall, discovering Kandy Lake is a fantastic way to recharge your spirit while immersing yourself in nature.

4. Experience Traditional Kandyan Dance: A Cultural Spectacle

One of the must-try Kandy Activities is witnessing the enchanting performance of Traditional Kandyan Dance. This unique art form is steeped in history and expresses the ancient rituals of Sri Lanka. The dance is characterized by elaborate costumes, rhythmic drumming, and intricate movements, often telling stories from local legend.

Moreover, many cultural shows are held every evening in Kandy. Attending one of these performances is an amazing way to immerse yourself in the local culture. Not only do you get to enjoy the captivating dances, but you also experience the vibrant energy of the performers. In addition, you can often participate in a brief lesson—even a short, lively dance session with the dancers!

Pro Tip: Make sure to check the schedule of performances, as they may vary throughout the year.

5. Visit the Kandy Market: A Feast for the Senses

Visiting the Kandy Market is undeniably one of the most exciting Kandy Activities. The market is bustling with life, filled with an array of vibrant spices, fresh produce, and handicrafts. Here, you can taste local delicacies and buy souvenirs to remember your trip.

As you navigate through the market’s aisles, you’ll encounter stalls selling everything from exotic fruits to intricate batik fabrics. Additionally, the warm and welcoming atmosphere makes it easy to connect with local vendors. While you shop, be sure to bargain; it’s a typical practice and adds to the fun!

Tip: Try the fresh fruit juices available at numerous stands—they are refreshingly delicious!

6. Hike to the Bahirawakanda Vihara: Stunning City Views

For those seeking adventure, hiking to Bahirawakanda Vihara is one of the top Kandy Activities to engage in. This stunning viewpoint offers panoramic vistas of the city and surrounding hills. The hike itself is moderate and takes around 30 minutes, making it suitable for all fitness levels.

As you ascend, you’ll encounter beautiful landscapes and the serene atmosphere that Sri Lanka is famous for. When you reach the top, be sure to explore the giant Buddha statue and the tranquil temple area. Furthermore, the views are simply breathtaking—perfect for snapping that Instagram-worthy photo!

Advice: Bring along plenty of water and perhaps a light snack to enjoy while you soak in the scenery.

7. Take a Scenic Train Ride from Kandy: Breathtaking Landscapes

One of the most unforgettable Kandy activities is taking a scenic train ride from Kandy to Ella. This journey is often celebrated as one of the most beautiful train rides in the world. As you glide through lush green hills, vibrant tea plantations, and enchanting waterfalls, you’ll quickly understand why this trip is a must-do.

Moreover, the train journey offers an incredible opportunity to soak in the natural beauty of Sri Lanka. The sound of the train clattering over the tracks complements the breathtaking views. Don’t forget your camera! The picturesque landscapes are simply irresistible and perfect for capturing memories.

To enhance your experience, consider traveling during the early morning hours or late afternoon to witness the sun casting magical hues over the hills. Clearly, this train ride provides a serene escape from the hustle and bustle and is highly recommended for all travelers visiting Kandy.

8. Swim in the Beautiful Udawattekele Sanctuary: Nature’s Haven

If you’re looking for a serene escape in Kandy, swimming in the Udawattekele Sanctuary should be on your list of Kandy activities. Nestled close to the city, this sanctuary is a natural haven filled with lush foliage, diverse wildlife, and tranquil lakes. It’s a great spot for nature lovers and those seeking adventure alike.

While swimming might not be the only activity here, the calm waters of the sanctuary offer a perfect opportunity for a refreshing dip. Remember, the serenity of the surroundings only enhances the experience. In addition to swimming, you can partake in hiking trails that wind through the sanctuary, which will allow you to observe various bird species and exotic plants.

Furthermore, while you’re there, take a moment to enjoy the peace and quiet that the sanctuary provides. The combination of swimming and exploring truly makes this a hidden gem in Kandy.

9. Enjoy a Local Cooking Class: Master Sri Lankan Flavors

One of the most delightful Kandy activities you can participate in is enjoying a local cooking class. Sri Lankan cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors and fragrant spices, and there’s no better way to appreciate this than by learning to cook it yourself. Local chefs are eager to share their culinary secrets with you!

During the class, you’ll have the opportunity to prepare traditional dishes such as rice and curry, hoppers, and various sambols. Not only will you learn how to cook these dishes, but you’ll also discover the cultural significance behind the ingredients. It’s an immersive experience that goes beyond just cooking.

Moreover, after the class, you get to savor the delicious meals you prepared. This hands-on activity not only satisfies your taste buds but also enriches your understanding of Sri Lankan culture. Engaging with locals and learning their cooking methods can create lasting memories, making this cooking class a highlight of your Kandy journey.

10. Experience the Esala Perahera Festival: A Vibrant Celebration

Experiencing the Esala Perahera Festival is undoubtedly one of the most thrilling Kandy activities to partake in. This grand festival, typically held in July or August, is a spectacular display of culture, faith, and tradition. As I wandered through the streets during this time, I was mesmerized by the vibrant parades filled with colorfully adorned elephants, dancers, and drummers.

Each night of the festival is dedicated to different processions, and the atmosphere is electric with excitement. Furthermore, the main highlight is the relic of the Tooth of Buddha, which is carried in a ceremonial casket by a beautifully decorated elephant, making for a breathtaking sight.

Notably, it’s essential to secure a good vantage point early, as the crowds tend to swell. Therefore, planning ahead can enhance your experience significantly.

“The Esala Perahera is not just a festival; it’s an embodiment of the rich cultural heritage of Sri Lanka.”

In conclusion, immersing yourself in the Esala Perahera is an unforgettable way to witness the local traditions of Kandy while enjoying a celebration that has been cherished for centuries.

Kandy is a city that offers a wide array of activities, enriching your travel experience and connecting you to its rich heritage. From immersing yourself in spiritual traditions at the Temple of the Sacred Tooth to enjoying the serene beauty of Kandy Lake, each activity provides a glimpse into the heart and soul of this enchanting city.
