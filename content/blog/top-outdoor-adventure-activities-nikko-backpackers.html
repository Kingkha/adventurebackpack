Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What are the best budget-friendly things to do in Nikkō for backpackers?

A: Nikkō offers plenty of free or low-cost activities for backpackers. Hiking various trails like the ones around Kirifuri Falls or Lake Chuzenji, exploring the free areas of the temples (like the Futarasan Shrine grounds), and simply enjoying the scenic natural landscapes are excellent options. Picnics amidst nature also save on food costs.

Q: Is Nikkō safe for solo female backpackers?

A: Yes, Japan, including Nikkō, is generally very safe for solo female travelers. Locals are helpful, and crime rates are low. However, always exercise common sense, especially when hiking solo on less-trafficked trails. Inform someone of your route, carry a map or GPS, and be aware of wildlife, though encounters are rare.

Q: What’s the best way to get around Nikkō for outdoor adventures?

A: For outdoor adventures, a combination of walking, local buses, and cycling is ideal. The Nikkō bus system connects major attractions and trailheads. Renting a bicycle near Nikkō Station or Lake Chuzenji offers flexibility for shorter distances. For remote trails, taxis can be an option, but they are more expensive.
