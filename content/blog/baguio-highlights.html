Baguio Highlights

Baguio highlights are a testament to its rich culture, cool climate, and stunning landscapes, making it a popular destination for both locals and tourists. Nestled in the mountains of the Philippines, Baguio City is known for its picturesque views, vibrant flower gardens, and bustling market scenes. In this article, we will explore the top 10 must-see attractions in Baguio that encapsulate the unique charm and diverse Activities this city has to offer. Whether you seek adventure, relaxation, or cultural immersion, you’ll find that Baguio is a destination that truly has it all.

1. Burnham Park: The Heart of Baguio’s Natural Beauty

Burnham Park, often regarded as the heart of Baguio’s natural beauty, is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to this scenic city. Established in 1903, this park offers a tranquil escape amidst the hustle and bustle of urban life. With its lush gardens, man-made lake, and vibrant flower beds, it’s a perfect spot for picnics and leisurely strolls.

Perhaps best of all, you can rent boats to paddle around the lake or simply relax under the shade of the trees. As you explore the park, don’t miss the iconic Burnham Lagoon, where families often gather. Furthermore, the park hosts various events that showcase Baguio’s local Culture. Therefore, whether you’re seeking relaxation or adventure, Burnham Park promises a delightful experience for all visitors.

2. Mines View Park: A Panoramic View of Nature’s Riches

Mines View Park offers one of the best panoramic views of the Cordillera mountains and the alluring scenery of Baguio. Located just a few kilometers from the city center, the park serves as an ideal spot for photography enthusiasts. The breathtaking sight of the distant mountains, combined with the cool, crisp air, creates a memorable experience.

Visitors often enjoy shopping for local souvenirs at the nearby stalls, featuring everything from handcrafted items to fresh produce. Additionally, be sure to try the region’s famous peanut brittle. As twilight descends, the park’s atmosphere transforms, making it a perfect backdrop for a romantic escape or peaceful reflection.

3. Session Road: The Vibrant Commercial Hub of Baguio

Session Road is undeniably the vibrant commercial hub of Baguio, bustling with life and energy. Stretching over a kilometer, it is lined with shops, restaurants, and cafés that offer a taste of local Culture. On this street, you can find everything from trendy boutiques to traditional crafts.

Moreover, if you’re a foodie, Session Road is the perfect place to indulge in Baguio’s culinary delights. For instance, there are numerous eateries serving local specialties, including the beloved strawberry taho and fresh vegetables. Furthermore, the lively atmosphere makes it an excellent spot to people-watch and soak in the local vibe. Regardless of your interests, this hub has something for everyone.

4. The Teacher’s Camp: A Historical Venue for Leisure

The Teacher’s Camp is a unique destination that offers a beautiful blend of history and leisure in Baguio. Established in the early 1900s, this site originally served as a training ground for teachers. Today, it stands as a serene retreat amidst the lush pine trees, attracting both locals and tourists.

As you wander through its grounds, you’ll discover a range of facilities that cater to relaxation and recreation. For example, you can find cozy cottages perfect for group outings. Additionally, the camp features wide-spanning lawns ideal for family picnics. Ultimately, it provides a perfect backdrop for bonding with family and friends.

Moreover, the peaceful atmosphere and beautiful scenery make it an excellent spot for physical Activities. Whether you prefer hiking or engaging in team sports, the Teacher’s Camp has something for everyone.

5. Camp John Hay: Nature and Adventure Combined

Camp John Hay is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to explore the natural beauty of Baguio. Nestled amidst towering pine trees, this former rest and recreation facility for the U.S. Armed Forces now serves as a public leisure park.

Visitors can engage in various outdoor Activities. For instance, there are numerous walking trails that reveal the stunning landscapes around the camp. Furthermore, the famous Bell House Museum provides an insight into the area’s rich history.

Once you finish exploring, you can unwind at the nearby picnic areas, or enjoy delicious food at the camp’s eateries. Notably, the cool climate coupled with breathtaking views ensures that every visit is memorable. Therefore, Camp John Hay should be on your list of Baguio highlights!

6. Baguio Botanical Garden: A Floral Wonderland

The Baguio Botanical Garden is one of the city’s most enchanting spots for nature lovers. Spanning over 20 hectares, this floral paradise boasts a diverse collection of plants, flowers, and trees that thrive in Baguio’s cooler climate.

Upon entering, you will immediately be greeted by vibrant hues and soothing scents. Each section of the garden reveals a different aspect of nature. Additionally, the walking paths lead you to themed areas like the zen gardens and native huts, offering a unique cultural experience too.

For families, this is an excellent place to let children play and explore. The park also hosts various seasonal events and flower festivals that highlight the beauty of local flora. As such, visiting the Baguio Botanical Garden not only connects you to nature but also enriches your travel experience.

7. Wright Park: Equestrian Adventures Await

Wright Park is truly a gem in Baguio, offering equestrian Adventures that captivate both locals and tourists alike. Known for its iconic pool of water with a stunning statue of a horse, this park invites visitors to experience the joy of horseback riding. Whether you are a beginner or an experienced rider, there’s something for everyone.

As I strolled through the park, I admired the lush trees and vibrant flowers, making it a perfect spot for relaxation. Riders can explore the surrounding areas under the guidance of knowledgeable staff. Furthermore, I recommend visiting early in the morning to enjoy a serene atmosphere and to avoid the crowd.

Wright Park not only offers equestrian Activities but also showcases the breathtaking scenery of the surrounding mountains.

8. The BenCab Museum: Artistic Journey Through Nature

The BenCab Museum is not just an art museum; it’s an experience that combines nature with Culture. Founded by renowned Filipino artist Benedicto Cabrera, the museum presents a unique collection of modern and indigenous art. Set against a backdrop of stunning views, the museum’s design blends seamlessly with its environment, making it a must-visit in Baguio.

While exploring, I was particularly taken by the diverse exhibits that highlight the arts of the Cordilleras. Moreover, the museum features a lovely café where you can savor local delicacies while overlooking the lush gardens. Don’t forget to explore the outdoor installations, which provide an artistic connection to the natural landscape.

9. Baguio Night Market: A Shopping Delight After Sunset

If you’re looking for a vibrant shopping experience, the Baguio Night Market is the place to be. Open from 9 PM to midnight, this bustling market transforms the streets of Harrison Road into a lively hub filled with local vendors. From tasty street food to unique souvenirs, there’s no shortage of incredible finds.

As I wandered through the market stalls, the aroma of street food lured me in, offering local favorites like ukoy and strawberry taho. Additionally, be prepared for some great bargain deals on clothing and accessories. The lively atmosphere, accompanied by local street performances, makes the night market a memorable experience.

Make sure to bring cash, as most vendors do not accept cards. Enjoy sampling local delicacies while shopping for unique items!

10. Strawberry Farm in La Trinidad: A Taste of Local Freshness

The Strawberry Farm in La Trinidad, just a short distance from Baguio, is an experience every traveler should savor. Visiting this farm not only allows you to enjoy the breathtaking views of the surrounding hills, but it also offers a delightful opportunity to taste fresh strawberries directly from the fields.

Typically, the best time to visit the farm is during the strawberry picking season, which runs from December to May. However, strawberries are available year-round in the farm’s market, so there’s always a chance to indulge in these sweet delights. As you stroll through the rows of vibrant plants, don’t forget to take a moment for some photo opportunities against the stunning backdrop of nature.

Pro Tip: Bring a basket and wear comfortable clothes, so you can fully enjoy strawberry picking!

Moreover, many local vendors sell various strawberry products. From jam to ice cream, you can taste the flavors of Baguio in every bite. Therefore, if you’re looking for an authentic local experience within Baguio’s highlights, a visit to the Strawberry Farm is a must!

Baguio highlights provide a wonderful array of experiences, from breathtaking views to vibrant markets that embody the city’s unique spirit. Each attraction offers a distinct taste of Baguio, whether it’s through nature, Culture, or cuisine.
