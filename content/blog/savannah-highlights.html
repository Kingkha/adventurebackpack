Savannah Highlights

Savannah highlights a perfect mixture of historical charm and modern vibrancy, making it one of the most enchanting cities in the United States. Known for its stunning architecture, picturesque parks, and rich cultural heritage, Savannah offers travelers an unforgettable experience. In this article, we will delve into the top 10 attractions that showcase the unique essence of Savannah, inviting you to explore the beauty and stories behind each location. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or a foodie, Savannah has something to enchant everyone.

1. Discover Savannah’s Historic Downtown: A Walk Through Time

Walking through Savannah’s Historic Downtown is like stepping back in time. The cobblestone streets, adorned with centuries-old oak trees draped in Spanish moss, create a picturesque backdrop for exploration. As you stroll, you’ll encounter stunning examples of 19th-century architecture that narrate tales of the past.

Highlights include the magnificent Savannah City Hall and the iconic Owens-Thomas House. Each building tells a story, making it crucial to take your time and appreciate the details. Interestingly, many tours offer insight into Savannah’s historical significance, enriching your visit with fascinating facts.

Therefore, don’t forget to bring your camera; every corner you turn is a perfect photo opportunity. Additionally, visit some local shops and cafes to experience the charm and local hospitality that Savannah is known for.

2. Forsyth Park: A Breath of Fresh Air in the City

Next on your itinerary should be Forsyth Park, a sprawling oasis in the heart of Savannah. This 30-acre park features lush greenery, beautiful trails, and monuments that make it a perfect escape from the urban hustle. Forsyth Fountain, a stunning centerpiece, is often surrounded by blooming flowers, making it an ideal spot for picnics or relaxation.

The park also hosts various Events throughout the year, such as farmers’ markets and music festivals. If you’re a fitness enthusiast, it’s a fantastic place for jogging or yoga amidst nature. In addition, the playground and recreational areas are perfect for family outings.

Overall, Forsyth Park encapsulates the serenity and beauty of Savannah, ensuring that visitors leave feeling refreshed and inspired.

3. Experience the Magic of River Street: Shopping and Dining by the Water

To experience Savannah’s vibrant atmosphere, head to River Street. This historic waterfront district is not only scenic but also offers a diverse array of shops and restaurants. As you wander along the cobblestone roads, you will encounter unique boutiques selling everything from handmade crafts to local artwork.

Moreover, dining along River Street provides the chance to taste delicious southern cuisine. Restaurants with outdoor seating let you enjoy your meal while taking in views of the Savannah River. If you’re lucky, you might even catch a glimpse of a majestic riverboat passing by. However, for a true local experience, try some fresh seafood at a riverside eatery.

In conclusion, River Street is a vibrant hub that perfectly captures the essence of Savannah’s charm and is a must-visit for every traveler.

4. Take a Stroll Through the Bonaventure Cemetery: Art and History Intertwined

The Bonaventure Cemetery is not just a resting place; it is a serene sanctuary filled with art and history. Located along the scenic banks of the Wilmington River, this cemetery offers visitors a glimpse into Savannah’s rich past. The ornate tombstones and sculpted monuments tell stories of those who once inhabited this charming city.

Moreover, as you wander through the oak-lined pathways, you’ll discover incredible works of art, such as the famous “Gracie Watson” monument. It’s also advisable to bring a camera; the picturesque landscapes make for stunning photographs. Additionally, the peaceful atmosphere offers a moment of reflection amidst the city’s hustle and bustle.

5. The Iconic Savannah Squares: A Nod to Southern Charm

Located throughout Savannah’s Historic District, the iconic squares are a hallmark of this vibrant city. Each square, such as Chippewa Square and Cluskey Square, is beautifully landscaped and equipped with benches, making it the perfect spot to relax and soak in the charm of Savannah. Furthermore, they serve as a connection between historical Landmarks and modern shops, which adds to their appeal.

Interestingly, these squares were originally designed as parks for social gatherings and community Events. As you stroll through them, you can almost feel the rich Culture and history surrounding you. Take the time to appreciate the fountains and statues that adorn these squares. They are not just parks; they are a testament to Savannah’s commitment to preserving its heritage.

6. Ghost Tours in Savannah: Uncover the Haunting Stories of the Past

When it comes to ghost tours, Savannah is known as one of the most haunted cities in America. Combing through the cobblestone streets at night, you can explore the spooky side of Savannah. These tours offer fascinating tales of ghostly encounters, and they provide a different perspective on the city’s history.

Moreover, you might visit sites like the Andrew Low House or the Laurel Grove Cemetery, both rich with eerie legends. Many tour guides are well-versed in local folklore, adding an extra layer of authenticity to your experience. If you’re feeling adventurous, embarking on a ghost tour will surely be one of the unforgettable Savannah highlights that you will cherish!

7. Savannah’s Cuisine: A Culinary Journey Through Southern Flavors

One of the most delightful aspects of visiting Savannah is undoubtedly its cuisine. Here, you can indulge in a culinary journey through a variety of Southern flavors that celebrate local ingredients and traditions. From the iconic shrimp and grits to the freshest seafood, the culinary scene is vibrant and rich.

Moreover, many restaurants offer outdoor seating, allowing you to enjoy your meal while soaking in the beautiful Savannah atmosphere. Garibaldi’s Cafe is a must-visit for its critical acclaim and outstanding dishes. Notably, the low-country boil is a local favorite, and you can find it at various eateries throughout the city.

To enhance your dining experience, don’t forget to pair your meal with a sweet Southern tea or the famous mint julep. Overall, Savannah’s culinary landscape is a delightful opportunity to immerse yourself in the local culture.

8. Cathedral of St. John the Baptist: A Marvel of Architecture

The Cathedral of St. John the Baptist stands as an architectural marvel and a centerpiece of Savannah’s historic district. This stunning cathedral, completed in the 19th century, showcases French Gothic style, with intricately detailed stained glass windows that cast colorful light throughout the sacred space.

When you visit, take a moment to admire the magnificent twin spires that reach elegantly towards the sky. Inside, the cathedral features beautiful wooden ceilings and exquisite altars that captivate visitors. Furthermore, the peaceful ambiance invites contemplation and reflection.

In addition, the cathedral frequently hosts services and community events, making it a lively part of Savannah’s cultural fabric. Therefore, a visit here offers not just beauty, but also a sense of the city’s spiritual life.

9. The Savannah History Museum: Delve into the City’s Rich Heritage

For those eager to Explore Savannah’s past, the Savannah History Museum is a treasure trove of information and artifacts. Located in what was once the passenger depot, this museum offers visitors a chance to delve into the city’s rich heritage. Exhibits showcase everything from Revolutionary War memorabilia to items related to the famous film “Forrest Gump,” which was filmed in various locations within Savannah.

Furthermore, the museum provides an insightful glimpse into the lives of the city’s residents throughout different historical periods. One of the highlights is the restored locomotive that speaks to Savannah’s role in transportation history.

To enhance your visit, consider joining one of the museum’s guided tours. These tours often provide fascinating stories and deeper insights that you would not get just from reading the displays. In conclusion, the Savannah History Museum is a must-see for understanding the context of this beautiful city.

10. Midnight in the Garden of Good and Evil Tour: Fiction Meets Reality

The Midnight in the Garden of Good and Evil Tour offers an intriguing glimpse into Savannah’s storied past, blending fact with fiction. Inspired by the bestselling book by John Berendt, this tour takes you to the very locations that played pivotal roles in the story of the notorious Savannah socialite Jim Williams.

As you stroll through the historical streets, you will hear tales of murder, mystery, and the captivating Culture that permeates the city. Along the way, the guide shares fascinating anecdotes that bring the characters to life. Furthermore, you will visit notable sites like the Mercer-Williams House and see the iconic locations that shaped the narrative.

“This tour is a must for anyone who loves a good story, with Savannah’s beauty as a backdrop.”

In conclusion, the Midnight in the Garden of Good and Evil Tour not only enriches your understanding of the book but also showcases Savannah’s alluring charm. So, prepare to be enchanted as you dive into a world where fiction meets reality!

Exploring the highlights of Savannah reveals a city rich in history, beauty, and Southern culture. From the enchanting squares that punctuate the landscape to the delicious local cuisine that tempts your taste buds, every corner of Savannah tells a story waiting to be discovered.
