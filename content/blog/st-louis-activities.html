St Louis Activities: Top 10 Things to Do in the City

St Louis is a vibrant city brimming with activities that cater to every type of traveler. From iconic landmarks to hidden gems, St Louis activities offer something unique for history buffs, art enthusiasts, and food lovers alike. In this article, we will unveil the top 10 must-do activities in St Louis, highlighting what makes this city a treasure trove of experiences. Prepare to explore the cultural richness, stunning architecture, and exhilarating Adventures that await you in the Gateway to the West.

1. Visit the Iconic Gateway Arch: A Symbol of St Louis Activities

The Gateway Arch is undoubtedly one of the most recognizable Landmarks in the United States, signifying the soul of St Louis. Standing at 630 feet, it is not just an architectural marvel; it represents the spirit of westward expansion. You can take a tram ride to the top, where breathtaking views of the city and the Mississippi River await.

Moreover, the surrounding park offers scenic pathways perfect for leisurely walks. In addition, the museum located at the base narrates the rich history of the region. Therefore, visiting the Gateway Arch is not just a sightseeing event; it’s an essential part of St Louis activities for anyone interested in Culture and history.

2. Explore Forest Park: Outdoor Activities and Natural Beauty

Forest Park is a stunning urban oasis and one of the largest urban parks in the U.S., covering more than 1,300 acres. Equally important, it hosts a variety of St Louis activities including picnicking, hiking, and cycling. You may also need to check out the numerous lakes and picturesque landscapes that make this park a favorite for both locals and visitors.

In addition to its natural beauty, Forest Park is home to several attractions like the St Louis Zoo and the Science Center. Notably, these offerings cater to families as well as nature enthusiasts. So, whether you desire a peaceful stroll or engaging outdoor games, Forest Park has something for everyone!

3. Discover the St Louis Art Museum: A Cultural Haven

The St Louis Art Museum is a mecca for art lovers and a significant part of St Louis activities. Housed in a stunning building within Forest Park, the museum boasts an impressive collection of over 33,000 artworks. You can explore pieces from various cultures, including European, American, and Asian art.

Additionally, the museum often hosts temporary exhibitions and educational programs that engage visitors. Furthermore, admission to the museum is free, making it accessible for all. If you’re passionate about art, this museum will surely enrich your St Louis experience!

4. Enjoy the Thrills at the St Louis Zoo: Family-Friendly Fun

One of the top St Louis activities perfect for families is a visit to the renowned St Louis Zoo. Situated in Forest Park, this amazing zoological park offers free admission, allowing you to explore a wide array of animal exhibits without breaking the bank. The zoo is home to over 18,000 animals, including everything from majestic elephants to playful penguins.

While wandering through the zoo, make sure to check out the Big Cat Country and River’s Edge. These exhibits provide an up-close look at some of the world’s most impressive creatures. Additionally, the Carousel and 4D theater offer fun, interactive experiences for children.

Moreover, guests can enjoy special events like live animal shows and feedings that highlight the zoo’s commitment to education and conservation. Overall, visiting the St Louis Zoo guarantees a day filled with excitement and learning for the whole family!

5. Indulge in Local Cuisine: Culinary Activities in St Louis

St Louis is not only known for its attractions but also for its vibrant culinary scene. Exploring local cuisine is one of the most delicious St Louis activities to engage in during your visit. From iconic toasted ravioli to mouthwatering gooey butter cake, the city presents flavors that will delight your palate.

To truly experience the local fare, consider dining at restaurants that serve St Louis-style barbecue. The tender, smoked meats, accompanied by a spicy sauce, will leave you craving more. Additionally, don’t miss out on trying the city’s unique take on the classic pizza, which features a thin crust and loads of cheese.

In conclusion, whether you’re dining at beloved eateries or exploring food festivals, indulging in local cuisine is an essential aspect of your St Louis experience!

6. Unwind at the Missouri Botanical Garden: Nature’s Paradise

For those seeking serenity and natural beauty, the Missouri Botanical Garden is a must-visit destination in St Louis. This stunning oasis is one of the oldest botanical gardens in the United States, showcasing a breathtaking array of plants, flowers, and landscapes. The garden spans over 79 acres, making it ideal for leisurely strolls among vibrant blooms.

While visiting, be sure to explore the Japanese Garden, which offers tranquil ponds and intricate landscaping that reflect traditional Japanese designs. Moreover, the Climatron® is a stunning geodesic dome housing diverse tropical plants, providing an escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.

In essence, the Missouri Botanical Garden not only enhances relaxation but also invites visitors to connect with nature. Therefore, it is undoubtedly a highlight among your St Louis activities to enjoy.

7. Experience the Magic of the City Museum: An Architectural Adventure

The City Museum in St Louis is a must-visit for anyone seeking unique activities that blend creativity and fun. This one-of-a-kind museum isn’t your typical building; it’s an architectural marvel crafted from repurposed architectural elements. To start your adventure, ascend its towers and slides that promise thrilling views and excitement.

What makes it truly special is its interactive art installations that captivate both children and adults alike. Moreover, visitors can explore secret tunnels, crawl through various structures, and even enjoy a rooftop Ferris wheel that overlooks the city. This dynamic space encourages exploration and play, making it an unforgettable experience. Therefore, don’t miss out on visiting the City Museum – it embodies the essence of St Louis activities in a way that’s both imaginative and exhilarating!

8. Discover History at the Old Courthouse: A Journey Back in Time

Step back in time by visiting the Old Courthouse in St Louis, where history comes alive through remarkable architecture and compelling stories. This historic site played a pivotal role in the famous Dred Scott case, making it a significant landmark for civil rights. As you walk through its grand halls, you’ll feel the weight of history surrounding you.

Guided tours are available and provide engaging insights into the courthouse’s past. Additionally, art exhibitions and educational programs further enhance your visit. In fact, the stunning dome and beautifully restored interiors are a feast for the eyes. Ultimately, this journey through the Old Courthouse connects you to St Louis’s rich heritage, making it a rewarding aspect of your St Louis activities.

9. Participate in the St Louis Cardinals Game: A Sports Enthusiast’s Dream

No visit to St Louis is complete without experiencing the excitement of a St Louis Cardinals game. As one of the most storied franchises in Major League Baseball, attending a game at Busch Stadium is a thrilling way to immerse yourself in St Louis Culture. The energy of the crowd and the smell of hot dogs create an atmosphere that is simply electrifying.

Moreover, fans can enjoy various pre-game festivities including live music and food vendors. It’s a lively and unforgettable way to bond with locals and fellow sports enthusiasts. Additionally, whether you’re cheering for home runs or taking in the stunning views of the city skyline, watching the Cardinals play is one of the premier activities in St Louis. So grab your glove and get ready for a memorable day at the ballpark!

10. Explore the Delmar Loop: A Hub for Entertainment and Activities

The Delmar Loop is a vibrant area in St. Louis that offers a unique blend of Culture, entertainment, and dining. When you visit this bustling district, you will be thrilled by the variety of activities available. From eclectic shops to live music venues, there’s something for everyone.

One of the Highlights is the Delmar Loop itself, a historic street lined with colorful storefronts and art installations. Moreover, this neighborhood is home to the famous St. Louis Walk of Fame, honoring local heroes from various fields such as music and sports. It’s an excellent opportunity for a casual stroll while soaking in the local Culture.

In addition to shopping and sightseeing, food lovers will appreciate the diverse culinary options. Don’t miss the chance to try some incredible local dishes at one of the many restaurants. Ultimately, the Delmar Loop exemplifies the lively spirit of St. Louis, making it a must-visit destination for anyone looking to dive into the city’s exciting atmosphere.

St Louis activities present an incredible opportunity to explore the city’s rich culture, history, and vibrant lifestyle.
