Oviedo Highlights

Oviedo, the capital of Asturias in northern Spain, is a city rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. With its stunning architecture, vibrant squares, and delicious cuisine, Oviedo offers travelers an unforgettable experience. In this article, we will delve into the top highlights of Oviedo, showcasing its historical Landmarks, local traditions, and hidden gems. Whether you’re a history buff, a foodie, or simply looking to soak in the local atmosphere, these Oviedo highlights are sure to make your visit memorable.

1. Discovering the Stunning Cathedral of San Salvador

The Cathedral of San Salvador is undoubtedly one of the Oviedo highlights that should not be missed. This magnificent cathedral, known for its unique blend of Gothic and Romanesque architecture, stands as a testament to the city’s rich history. As you enter, your eyes will be drawn to the intricately designed interiors, particularly the famous Camara Santa, a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Moreover, the views from the bell tower offer a breathtaking panorama of the city. While visiting this historical landmark, consider joining a guided tour to learn about its fascinating stories and architectural secrets. Pro tip: Arriving early in the morning allows you to enjoy a quieter experience while capturing stunning photographs.

2. Strolling Through the Historic Old Town of Oviedo

The Historic Old Town of Oviedo is a delightful area to explore, filled with narrow cobblestone streets and stunning architecture. As you wander through its charming alleys, you’ll come across historical Landmarks, quaint shops, and delightful plazas. One of the most notable sites is the Plaza del Fontán, which is a great place to grab a coffee and soak in the ambiance.

Furthermore, don’t forget to look for the famous Statues of Mother Teresa and Fruela I, which add a whimsical touch to the area. Aside from the sights, this neighborhood is perfect for discovering local shops, where you can find unique handmade crafts. In addition, exploring at sunset gives the Old Town a magical glow.

3. Savoring Traditional Asturian Cuisine: A Culinary Tour

When visiting Oviedo, indulging in traditional Asturian cuisine is one of the must-do experiences. From hearty Fabada Asturiana (bean stew) to fresh cachopo (breaded meat dish), there’s a plethora of dishes to tantalize your taste buds. A culinary tour in Oviedo not only satisfies your hunger but also introduces you to the cultural significance of each dish.

Moreover, consider visiting local sidrerías (cider houses) to learn about the art of pouring sidra, a traditional cider unique to the region. As you savor these delicacies, you’re also diving into the rich gastronomic Culture of Asturias. To enhance your culinary adventure, look for guided food tours that provide insights into the best local eateries.

4. Visiting the Archaeological Museum of Asturias

The Archaeological Museum of Asturias provides a fascinating glimpse into the region’s rich history. With artifacts dating back to prehistoric times, this museum showcases the evolution of human civilization in Asturias. As I entered, I was immediately drawn to the intricate displays that narrate stories of ancient cultures.

One highlight is the collection of Roman mosaics, which beautifully reflect the artistry of that era. Additionally, the museum offers informative panels in multiple languages, making it accessible for all visitors. Many travelers recommend taking a guided tour, as it enriches the experience with in-depth knowledge about the exhibits.

5. Exploring the Charming Mercado El Fontán

Mercado El Fontán is a bustling market that captures the essence of local life in Oviedo. When I visited, the vibrant atmosphere was palpable, with stalls brimming with fresh produce, artisanal cheeses, and traditional Asturian products. It’s a perfect spot to immerse yourself in the local Culture.

Moreover, be sure to try the “pío pío” pastries and freshly made “empanadas.” Transitioning between different stalls, I enjoyed chatting with friendly vendors who were eager to share their culinary secrets. For those seeking authentic souvenirs, this market is a treasure trove of unique finds.

6. Enjoying the Views from Santa Catalina Park

Santa Catalina Park offers some of the most stunning views in Oviedo. Nestled on a hill, the park is an ideal spot for a relaxing afternoon. As I strolled through the lush greenery, I was captivated by the panoramic views of the city and surrounding mountains.

The park also features striking sculptures that add to its charm. Notably, the “Eulogy of the Horizon” stands as a must-see monument. Many locals frequent this park, especially during sunset, to unwind and soak up the breathtaking scenery. Certainly, a visit here elevates your Oviedo highlights experience.

7. Unveiling the Artistry at the Museo de Bellas Artes de Asturias

The Museo de Bellas Artes de Asturias is a treasure trove of artistic creations. Located in the heart of Oviedo, it houses an impressive collection of works spanning several centuries, showcasing both local and international artists. Upon entering, you are immediately welcomed by a variety of styles, including Renaissance, Baroque, and modern art.

In particular, the museum features illustrious works by Asturian artists, which highlight the region’s rich cultural heritage. Notably, the museum’s architecture itself is a fusion of classic and contemporary design. Therefore, it doesn’t just serve as a gallery but also as a remarkable building worth exploring.

When visiting, be sure to check the exhibition schedule for temporary displays. This may enhance your experience, introducing you to new talents and fascinating artworks. Don’t miss the chance to understand Oviedo’s artistic landscape, as it is a significant part of Oviedo Highlights.

8. Experiencing Local Festivals: A Glimpse into Oviedo’s Culture

Experiencing the local festivals in Oviedo provides a vibrant insight into the region’s Culture. Throughout the year, the city welcomes various celebrations, the most famous being Las Fallas and San Mateo. These festivals not only celebrate tradition but also bring together locals and tourists in joyous festivities.

During Las Fallas, large papier-mâché figures take center stage. Streets fill with color, music, and laughter as festivities culminate in an impressive display of fireworks. In contrast, the San Mateo Festival, celebrated every September, showcases music, gastronomy, and local traditions. Food stalls line the streets, allowing you to savor the best Asturian cuisine.

It’s recommended to plan your visit around these Events. This way, you can experience Oviedo Highlights through its lively and rich community atmosphere.

9. Adventuring in Covadonga: Nature and Pilgrimage

Just a short drive from Oviedo lies Covadonga, a stunning blend of nature and pilgrimage. Renowned for its majestic landscapes and sacred sites, Covadonga is a must-visit for travelers seeking adventure. The picturesque lakes, known as Los Lagos de Covadonga, are surrounded by the Picos de Europa mountains, making it an ideal spot for hiking and sightseeing.

Moreover, the Basilica of Covadonga is iconic; it stands as a shrine dedicated to the Virgin of Covadonga. This sacred site holds historical significance, attracting countless pilgrims each year. Exploring the surrounding park is not just a chance to connect with nature but also to delve into the legends steeped in Spanish history.

When adventuring to Covadonga, I suggest taking a guided tour. This will enrich your experience with stories and insights about both the natural and cultural significance of the area. Thus, it becomes one of the essential Oviedo Highlights that shouldn’t be missed on your journey.

10. Relishing Sidra Tasting in Asturias: The Traditional Cider

When you think of Asturias, one of the highlights you cannot miss is its renowned sidra, or traditional cider. This unique beverage is not just a drink; it is an integral part of Asturian Culture and lifestyle. Sidra is made from local apples, and the production process is a fascinating experience on its own.

Many local cider houses, known as “sidrerías”, offer tastings, where you can savor the different varieties of this crisp and refreshing beverage. As you enjoy your sidra, don’t forget to observe the traditional way it is poured from a height, a method that aerates the drink, enhancing its flavors.

If you want a true local experience, try pairing sidra with some traditional Asturian tapas such as chorizo a la sidra or queso de Cabrales. To truly appreciate Oviedo highlights, consider joining a guided sidra tour, which will immerse you in the history and craftsmanship behind this beloved drink.

Oviedo is a city that beautifully blends history, art, and culinary delights. From the magnificent Cathedral of San Salvador to the lively Mercado El Fontán, each highlight contributes to the rich tapestry of life in Oviedo. Whether you’re indulging in Asturian sidra or exploring the historic streets, these experiences will allow you to connect with the essence of this enchanting city.
