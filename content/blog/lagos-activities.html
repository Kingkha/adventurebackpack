Lagos Activities: Top 10 Unique Things to Do

Lagos activities are as diverse and vibrant as the city itself, making it one of the top travel destinations in Africa. With a rich blend of culture, history, and modernity, Lagos offers a plethora of experiences that cater to every type of traveler. From bustling markets and mesmerizing art scenes to serene beaches and exciting nightlife, each activity provides an opportunity to dive into the heart of this dynamic city. In this article, we’ll explore 10 unique Lagos activities that will immerse you in the local culture and offer unforgettable memories.

1. Explore the Vibrant Markets: A Shopper’s Paradise

If you are searching for an authentic Lagos experience, then visiting the vibrant markets is a must. Markets like Balogun Market and Lekki Market offer a treasure trove of local crafts, textiles, and food. As you stroll through, you’ll encounter a melange of colors, scents, and sounds that capture the essence of Lagos.

Moreover, bargaining is an integral part of shopping here. To make the most of your experience, always negotiate prices with a smile! This way, you not only score a great deal but also engage with the friendly vendors.

Pro Tip: Visit early in the morning for a more relaxed shopping experience and to avoid the crowds.

2. Discover the Rich History at National Museum Lagos

The National Museum Lagos is the perfect destination for history buffs wanting to dive into Nigeria’s rich cultural heritage. Housing fascinating artifacts, including the renowned Benin Bronzes, the museum offers a comprehensive overview of Nigeria’s history and traditions.

While exploring the museum, you’ll find well-curated exhibitions that tell the story of Nigeria’s diverse cultures. Additionally, guided tours can enhance your visit, providing insights you might miss when exploring solo.

Ultimately, a trip to the National Museum is not just educational; it is a profound journey through time.

3. Relax at Tarkwa Bay: A Hidden Beach Gem

If you seek a serene escape from the bustling city, head to Tarkwa Bay. This hidden beach gem is easily accessible by boat and offers a peaceful environment perfect for relaxation. Unlike other crowded beaches, Tarkwa Bay boasts soft sands and gentle waves, ideal for swimming and sunbathing.

Also, you can enjoy various water sports, including jet skiing and surfing. If you are looking for a lively atmosphere, weekend beach parties are a great way to socialize with both locals and visitors. Don’t forget to bring some snacks and a good book to fully enjoy your beach day!

4. Enjoy the Thrilling Nightlife at Victoria Island

When it comes to lively Lagos activities, Victoria Island stands out as a prime destination for nightlife enthusiasts. The area boasts a plethora of upscale bars, nightclubs, and lounges that come alive after sunset. Notably, many venues feature live music and DJ sets that keep the party going well into the early hours.

For instance, the rooftop bars offer stunning views of the city skyline, making for a perfect backdrop as you sip your favorite cocktails. Moreover, the vibrant atmosphere is enhanced by the diverse crowd, ranging from locals to international travelers.

As you embark on your nightlife adventure, remember to dress to impress, as it’s common for venues to have a chic dress code. Therefore, if you enjoy dancing or simply mingling with new people, Victoria Island’s nightlife will not disappoint.

5. Indulge in Local Cuisine: A Culinary Adventure

No exploration of Lagos would be complete without indulging in its rich culinary landscape. Lagos offers an exciting array of traditional Nigerian dishes, with each meal telling a story of culture and history. One must-try dish is jollof rice, a flavorful staple often served with grilled chicken or fish.

Transitioning into street food, be sure to sample suya, which are spicy beef skewers that are beloved by locals. Additionally, visiting local restaurants like Terra Kulture not only serves delicious food but also showcases the essence of Nigerian art and music.

To enrich your culinary adventure, consider taking a food tour. Such tours provide a deeper insight into the origins of dishes while you taste various local delicacies. Thus, indulging in the local cuisine is indeed one of the top Lagos activities you shouldn’t miss.

6. Experience Art and Culture at Nike Art Gallery

For travelers looking to immerse themselves in Nigerian culture, the Nike Art Gallery is a must-visit destination. The gallery, founded by the renowned artist Nike Davies-Okundaye, showcases an extensive collection of Nigerian art, ranging from paintings to textiles.

Moreover, transitioning from one exhibit to another, visitors can appreciate the vibrant colors and intricate designs that enrich the art scene. During my visit, I was particularly impressed by the traditional beadwork and unique sculptures that reflect Nigeria’s rich heritage.

The gallery also offers workshops and cultural classes, allowing you to engage directly with local artisans. Consequently, this is a wonderful opportunity to learn more about the artistic techniques used in creating these masterpieces. Altogether, visiting the Nike Art Gallery is a fulfilling addition to any itinerary focused on Lagos activities.

7. Take a Boat Tour of the Lagos Lagoon

One of the most stunning Lagos activities is undoubtedly a boat tour of the Lagos Lagoon. This adventure allows you to soak in the breathtaking views of the city’s skyline while gliding over calm waters. Moreover, the lagoon connects various islands, offering a unique perspective on Lagos’s beauty.

As you sail, you will notice vibrant local life along the shores. Keep your camera ready, as you’ll want to capture the picturesque scenes. Transitioning from the hustle and bustle of the city to the serene atmosphere of the lagoon is a refreshing experience.

For the best experience, opt for a guided tour that includes stops at key points of interest. These may include local fishing villages or unspoiled beaches. In conclusion, a boat tour is an unforgettable way to appreciate the natural beauty of Lagos.

8. Participate in the Kalakuta Museum Experience

The Kalakuta Museum is a must-visit for anyone looking to delve into the rich cultural history of Nigeria. Located in the heart of Lagos, this museum is dedicated to the life of the legendary Fela Kuti, the father of Afrobeat music. Participating in the Kalakuta Museum Experience allows you to learn about Fela’s impact on music and society.

As you explore the museum, you can view personal artifacts, photographs, and even music recordings. Experiencing the stories and struggles of Fela will deepen your understanding of the cultural significance of his work.

Furthermore, guided tours are available that provide valuable insights into the museum’s exhibits. Ultimately, a visit to the Kalakuta Museum is an enriching experience that highlights the artistic spirit of Lagos.

9. Attend the Annual Lagos Carnival: A Festive Extravaganza

Every year, Lagos comes alive during the annual Lagos Carnival, which showcases the city’s vibrant culture and traditions. This event is one of the most celebrated Lagos activities, attracting thousands of locals and tourists alike. Typically held in April, the carnival features colorful parades, live music, and captivating performances.

Participants dress in extravagant costumes, reflecting the diverse heritage of Nigeria. As you witness the festivities, you’ll surely feel the energy and excitement in the air. The combination of rhythm, dance, and vibrant visuals creates an unforgettable atmosphere.

Additionally, food stalls offer a variety of local dishes, ensuring that your taste buds are not left out of the celebration. Thus, attending the Lagos Carnival is a fantastic way to immerse yourself in the culture while enjoying a festive extravaganza.

10. Go on a Wildlife Safari at Lekki Conservation Centre

If you are seeking an unforgettable experience in Lagos, look no further than the Lekki Conservation Centre. This stunning wildlife reserve offers visitors the chance to explore the diverse flora and fauna of Nigeria. Furthermore, it is home to the longest canopy walkway in Africa, providing breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

As you stroll along the walkway, keep an eye out for various wildlife species, including monkeys and different bird species. This unique experience allows you to immerse yourself in nature while also enjoying a bit of adventure. Additionally, the conservation centre is dedicated to the protection of endangered species and plays a significant role in local wildlife conservation efforts.

After your aerial adventure, take time to enjoy the picnic areas under serene canopies. Not only will you relax, but you can also relish the day while surrounded by nature. For families and nature lovers, visiting the Lekki Conservation Centre is undoubtedly one of the top Lagos activities.

The diverse range of Lagos activities ensures that there is something for everyone to enjoy, whether you’re looking for cultural enrichment, relaxation, or adventure. By indulging in these unique experiences, you’ll not only create lifelong memories but also gain a deeper appreciation for the vibrancy and uniqueness of Lagos.
