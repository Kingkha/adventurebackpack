Surabaya Itinerary: Your Ultimate Guide to Exploring the City

Surabaya, Indonesia’s vibrant second-largest city, is rich in history and modern charm. From its colonial architecture to bustling markets and delicious street food, a well-planned itinerary ensures you experience the best the city has to offer. Whether you’re keen to explore historic sites, indulge in local cuisine, or immerse yourself in the Culture, this Surabaya itinerary will guide you through an unforgettable adventure.

Key Takeaways

  • Day 1: Explore the House of Sampoerna, visit Surabaya’s Heroes Monument, and wander through the Old Town.
  • Day 2: Sample local street food at G-Walk, try the famous rawon (beef soup), and enjoy a coffee at Taman Bungkul.
  • Day 3: Visit the Surabaya Zoo, explore the vibrant Pasar Atum flea market, and enjoy the unique architecture of the Surabaya Grand Mosque.
  • Day 4: Take a day trip to Mount Bromo for breathtaking views or visit the historic city of Malang.
  • Day 5: Relax at Wonorejo Eco Park, explore the mangrove forest, and unwind at the beach on Kenjeran Island.

Day 1: Discover Historical Surabaya

Your Surabaya itinerary kicks off with a journey into the rich history of this vibrant city. Begin your explorations in the morning at the House of Sampoerna, a museum housed in a historical Dutch colonial building. This site offers deep insight into Indonesia’s tobacco industry while showcasing intriguing displays of the production process and the rich heritage of the Sampoerna family. Guided tours are available and highly recommended for a firsthand look at Surabaya’s history.

“Visiting the House of Sampoerna is like stepping back in time, experiencing the intertwining of heritage and commerce in Surabaya.”

Next, take a stroll to the Heroes Monument (Tugu Pahlawan), which stands as a tribute to the bravery of the city’s inhabitants during certain crucial historical Events. The monument is surrounded by beautiful gardens and offers a reflective space to contemplate the struggles and triumphs of the past. The memorial is a key element of the city’s identity and is often highlighted in Surabaya itineraries.

Enjoy your lunch at a nearby café before wandering through the Old Town, a charming area that showcases various colonial-era buildings and picturesque streets. Here, the mix of history and local life gives you a sense of what Surabaya once was and how it has evolved. Pay special attention to the Chinese heritage Street, colored with vibrant shops and traditional markets where you can dive into local Culture.

Afternoon Excursion: Kembang Jepun

In the afternoon, head to Kembang Jepun, known for its blend of heritage and modernity. This area features well-preserved architecture from different periods, and you can see local street life unfold here. As you walk through, don’t miss the opportunity to try some local coffee at one of the quaint cafés. Taman Bungkul is an excellent choice for a caffeine boost while enjoying the vibrant atmosphere.

Evening: Closing the Day

As the day winds down, consider dining at a local restaurant that offers traditional Indonesian dishes. This gives you the perfect chance to sample some local delicacies and reflect on your day in Surabaya. You might want to try dishes like nasi goreng (fried rice) or ikan bakar (grilled fish), which can often be found in many eateries around town.

Finally, take a leisurely evening walk along the Surabaya River, where you can enjoy the calm ambiance against a backdrop of twinkling lights from the city. It’s a perfect way to wrap up your historical exploration of Surabaya, setting the stage for the culinary Adventures that await you on Day 2.

Day 2: Culinary Delights and Street Food Exploration

Start your second day in Surabaya with a culinary adventure that showcases the rich and diverse flavors of Indonesian cuisine. Surabaya is renowned for its vibrant street food scene, making it a perfect destination for food lovers. This guide will lead you through some of the city’s most tantalizing street food offerings and culinary spots.

9am: Breakfast at Taman Bungkul

Begin your day at Taman Bungkul, a beautiful park that doubles as a meeting point for locals. Here, you can grab a traditional breakfast. Don’t miss out on trying nasi goreng (fried rice) or kerupuk (crackers) paired with a delicious cup of coffee. Taman Bungkul is famous for its vibrant atmosphere, filled with people enjoying their morning routines.

Pro Tip: This park is also an excellent spot for people-watching, so take a moment to relax after your meal and immerse yourself in the local Culture.

11am: G-Walk Culinary Center

After breakfast, head to G-Walk, a culinary center recognized for its extensive array of street food vendors. This lively area boasts numerous stalls offering various local delicacies. Here, you must taste rawon, a rich and flavorful beef soup made with black nutmeg and served with rice. Its distinctive flavor profile makes it a signature dish of East Java.

Rawon Black beef soup served with rice Highly Recommended!
Sate Surabaya Grilled skewers with peanut sauce Delicious and must-try!

As you indulge in these delectable offerings, be sure to savor the vibrant ambiance — music, laughter, and the sizzling sounds of food cooking fill the air.

2pm: Coffee and Snacks

Following your culinary excursion, visit one of the popular coffee shops in Surabaya. Enjoy a refreshing drink at a local café, where you can unwind with a relaxing atmosphere. Opt for a classic Indonesian kopi (coffee) or try a sweet es kopi susu (iced coffee with milk) while sampling local snacks, such as pisang goreng (fried bananas) or cake lapis (layer cake).

“Food is an integral part of Surabaya’s culture, and each bite tells a story of its diverse heritage.”

4pm: Explore Local Markets

In the late afternoon, venture to explore a local market, perhaps Pasar Atum. This bustling flea market is famous for its vibrant atmosphere, where you can find everything from fashion to handicrafts. While browsing, don’t forget to try some fresh fruits, like juicy mangos or rambutan from one of the numerous fruit vendors.

As the day draws to a close, take time to appreciate the flavors you’ve experienced. Whether it’s street food or local delicacies, each experience enriches your Surabaya itinerary.

Evening: Dinner and Night Market

End your day by visiting one of Surabaya’s night markets. Enjoy dinner at a food stall, choosing from various options that showcase the best of Surabaya’s culinary delights. These markets create a lively environment filled with an assortment of food choices and live entertainment, making it a great place to mingle with locals and other travelers.

In conclusion, your second day in Surabaya will undoubtedly be a feast for your senses, filled with culinary discoveries and the vibrant culture of the city. Don’t forget to carry your camera; you will want to capture every moment of this delectable adventure!

Day 3: Cultural attractions and Local Markets

On Day 3 of your Surabaya itinerary, immerse yourself in the rich cultural heritage of this vibrant city while exploring local markets. This day promises a blend of nature, history, and the essence of daily life in Surabaya.

Morning: Visit the Surabaya Zoo

Start your day early with a visit to the Surabaya Zoo, one of the oldest and largest zoos in Southeast Asia. Established in 1916, this zoo is home to over 3,000 animals representing diverse species from Indonesia and beyond. Walking through the lush, landscaped grounds, you’ll encounter rare animals such as the Sumatran tiger and various exotic birds.

While engaging with the animals, don’t miss the opportunity to learn about conservation efforts that the zoo is involved in. Exhibits often include educational information, making it both a fun and enriching experience for visitors of all ages.

Late Morning: Explore Pasar Atum Flea Market

Next, head over to Pasar Atum, a bustling flea market that showcases local culture and craftsmanship. Here, you can find an array of products, from traditional textiles and batik to handcrafted souvenirs and delicious snacks. The market is an ideal spot to shop for gifts and indulge in some bargaining which adds an exciting touch to your shopping experience.

Be sure to take your time wandering through the myriad of stalls and sampling local snacks such as kerupuk (crackers) and pisang molen (banana pastry). The aromatic spices and local ingredients will give you a taste of the area’s culinary vibrancy.

Lunch: Take a Break and Energize

After a morning of exploration, it’s time to refuel. Head to a nearby local eatery to enjoy some traditional dishes. One must-try meal is the rawon, a rich and flavorful beef soup characterized by its deep black color, which comes from the use of keluak nuts. Pair it with steamed rice and a side of fried tempeh for a satisfying meal.

“Food is an integral part of understanding a culture. In Surabaya, each dish tells a story of heritage and tradition.”

Afternoon: Surabaya Grand Mosque

Post-lunch, visit the Surabaya Grand Mosque, the largest mosque in Indonesia. Known for its stunning architecture, the mosque combines traditional Javanese and Middle Eastern designs. Witness the beauty of its intricate murals and massive domes as you stroll through the grounds. If you’re interested, guided tours are often available to share the history of this remarkable structure.

Take a moment to reflect and appreciate the serene atmosphere that surrounds this sacred space, making it a highly recommended stop on your Surabaya itinerary.

Evening: Local Dinner and Cultural Enrichment

Conclude your cultural adventure with dinner at a local restaurant where you can sample more traditional dishes. Opt for a restaurant that offers live music or cultural performances to further enrich your experience. This is an excellent opportunity to mingle with locals and gain insight into Surabaya’s rich heritage.

By the end of the day, you will have a deeper understanding of Surabaya’s culture, architecture, and the lively atmosphere of its markets. This day is not just a series of Activities; it’s a journey into the heart of the local culture.

Day 4: Day Trips Around Surabaya

On your fourth day of the Surabaya itinerary, take advantage of the stunning destinations closer to this vibrant city. Surabaya is your gateway to various day trips that promise breathtaking scenery, cultural Experiences, and adventure. Among the most popular options are a trip to Mount Bromo and the historical town of Malang.

Mount Bromo: Nature’s Marvel

Start early in the morning to embark on an unforgettable journey to Mount Bromo. This iconic volcano, part of the Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park, is about a two- to three-hour drive from Surabaya.

As you ascend, you’ll encounter stunning views of the Tengger Caldera. The best experience awaits at dawn when you can witness a mesmerizing sunrise over the volcano. Pro Tip: Arrive early to secure a prime view spot at one of the designated lookout points.

After the sunrise, hike or take a Jeep ride to the crater, where you can observe the smoking caldera up close. The otherworldly landscape, with its vast sea of sand and ethereal charm, provides the perfect backdrop for breathtaking photos.

Malang: A Historical and Cultural Gem

If you prefer a blend of history and culture, consider visiting Malang, a charming city known for its colonial architecture and cooler climate. Located approximately two hours from Surabaya, Malang offers a variety of attractions, including:

  • Malang Kota Tua: Wander through the bustling old town filled with Dutch colonial buildings, quaint cafes, and vibrant street art.
  • Kampung Warna Warni: Explore this colorful village that features houses painted in an array of vibrant colors, creating a photographer’s paradise.
  • Jami’ Mosque and Singosari Temple: Immerse yourself in the rich cultural history by visiting these impressive Landmarks.

Tips for Day Trips

When planning your day trips from Surabaya, consider the following tips for a smooth experience:

  • Transportation: Rent a car or hire a driver for a comfortable journey. Alternatively, join a tour group that offers package deals to significant locations.
  • Timing: Start your journey early in the day to make the most of your time at each destination. Especially for Mount Bromo, leaving around midnight is advised to catch the stunning sunrise.
  • Pack Essentials: Bring along water, snacks, comfortable shoes, a camera, and sun protection to ensure you are well-prepared for your Adventures.

Cozy up to the charming atmosphere of Surabaya’s surroundings. Whether you choose Mount Bromo’s breathtaking views or the cultural rich tapestry of Malang, these day trips will enhance your Surabaya itinerary and create lasting memories.

Day 5: Natural Wonders and Relaxation

On your final day in Surabaya, immerse yourself in the city’s natural wonders while also finding time to unwind. This day is perfect for balancing adventure with relaxation, making it a memorable conclusion to your Surabaya itinerary.

9:00 AM: Explore Wonorejo Eco Park

Start your day by visiting Wonorejo Eco Park, a sprawling green space that showcases the beauty of Surabaya’s natural landscape. This park provides a sanctuary for flora and fauna, making it a delightful spot for nature enthusiasts. Enjoy the vibrant landscapes, lush gardens, and tranquil surroundings. You can walk, jog, or simply relax on one of the park benches while enjoying the sounds of nature.

Pro Tip: Bring a camera to capture the stunning photos, especially early in the morning when the light is just right.

11:00 AM: Discover the Mangrove Forest

After your time at the eco park, head to the Mangrove Forest located in the northern part of the city. This lush area offers a unique ecosystem that is crucial for coastal protection and biodiversity. Wander along the wooden walkways, observe the diverse bird species, and take in the serene ambiance of this ecological treasure.

This ecological site not only provides educational opportunities but also allows for some recreational Activities such as bird watching and photography. It’s a chance to appreciate the local wildlife and the importance of conserving these natural habitats.

1:00 PM: Lunch at Kenjeran Beach

After exploring nature, make your way to Kenjeran Beach for lunch. This beautiful beach area is famous for its seafood restaurants that serve freshly caught dishes. Indulge in local specialties while enjoying ocean views. Don’t miss out on trying the grilled fish and other traditional Javanese seafood recipes—it’s a culinary experience that you won’t forget!

3:00 PM: Beachside Relaxation

After lunch, spend your afternoon unwinding at Kenjeran Beach. The beach is known for its golden sands and calm waters, perfect for swimming or simply soaking up the sun. There are plenty of beachside cafes and vendors where you can grab a light snack or a refreshing drink.

Notable Activities:

  • Sunset Viewing: Stay until evening for a breathtaking sunset.
  • Photography: Capture stunning coastal views at golden hour.

5:00 PM: Conclude the Day at Kenjeran Park

As the day winds down, you can opt to visit Kenjeran Park located close by. This vibrant park features a variety of family-friendly attractions, including amusement rides and a beautiful waterfront area. It’s an excellent way to wrap up your time in Surabaya.

In conclusion, Day 5 of your Surabaya itinerary is designed to help you experience the natural beauty and relaxation that this vibrant city offers. Whether you’re exploring the mangrove forests or enjoying fresh seafood at the beach, cherish the moments that connect you to Surabaya’s stunning landscapes.

Best Time to Visit: Seasonal Tips for Your Surabaya Itinerary

Planning your Surabaya itinerary requires an understanding of the best times to visit this vibrant city. Surabaya Experiences a tropical climate, characterized by two main seasons: the dry season and the wet season. Each season offers unique Experiences, so let’s delve into the details.

Dry Season (April to October)

The dry season is generally regarded as the best time to visit Surabaya, especially if you plan to explore outdoor attractions. From April to October, the weather is sunny and humidity levels are lower, making it a comfortable time for sightseeing.

  • April-May: This is an excellent time to visit as the weather is warm but not too hot. Popular Events include the Surabaya Flower Carnival, which takes place in May, featuring vibrant parades and beautiful floats.
  • June-August: These months are great for beach outings, given that it rarely rains. The temperature during the day averages around 30°C (86°F), ideal for outdoor activities.
  • September-October: As the dry season comes to an end, you can expect fewer tourists, making it a perfect time to enjoy popular attractions without the crowds.

During the dry months, outdoor excursions such as visiting the Surabaya Zoo or taking a walk in Wonorejo Eco Park are quite enjoyable. Moreover, an evening stroll along Kenjeran Beach is a must-do!

Wet Season (November to March)

Conversely, the wet season, which runs from November through March, brings increased rainfall and higher humidity. While it is possible to visit Surabaya during this time, keep in mind that occasional downpours may impact outdoor plans.

However, the wet season does not mean a lack of activities. It provides a wonderful opportunity to visit local markets like Pasar Atum and explore cultural sites indoors, such as the Surabaya Grand Mosque. Additionally, savoring local coffee at cozy spots like Taman Bungkul makes for a delightful way to spend a rainy afternoon.

Pro Tip: Always carry an umbrella or raincoat during the wet season and keep an eye on the weather forecasts to plan your outdoor activities effectively.

Wrapping Up

To sum up, when planning your Surabaya itinerary, consider visiting during the dry season for comfortable weather and outdoor advancements. However, if you enjoy fewer tourists and don’t mind the rain, the wet season can be equally rewarding.

Travel Tips for Your Surabaya Itinerary: Getting Around and Cost

When planning your Surabaya itinerary, understanding how to navigate this bustling city efficiently can greatly enhance your travel experience. Moreover, knowing about the associated costs will help you budget wisely. This guide provides you with essential travel tips for getting around Surabaya and managing your expenses.

Getting Around Surabaya

Surabaya is a vibrant city with various transportation options. Here are the most common methods to traverse its streets:

  • Taxis: Taxis in Surabaya are readily available and are a reliable option for getting around. Make sure to choose bluebird taxis, as they are considered safe and use a meter, ensuring you don’t overpay.
  • Ojek (Motorbike Taxi): For a quicker route through traffic, you can opt for an ojek. Use ride-sharing apps like Gojek or Grab to book a ride conveniently from your smartphone.
  • Public Buses: Surabaya has a bus network known as BATIK (Bus Trans Jawa Timur). These buses connect major areas in the city at an affordable fare, making it a good option for budget travelers.
  • Car Rentals: If you prefer driving yourself, renting a car is a viable choice. Various rental agencies are available. Keep in mind, however, that traffic can be intense, especially during rush hours.

Cost of Transportation

Understanding the typical costs associated with transportation will help you manage your travel budget effectively. Here’s an approximate breakdown:

Budgeting Tips

To get the most out of your Surabaya experience without breaking the bank, consider these budgeting tips:

  • Plan Your Itinerary: Allocating specific days for certain activities can help manage costs. Prioritize free attractions to balance your spending on transportation and food.
  • Eat Like a Local: Street food is not only delicious but also economical. Be sure to try local delicacies found in street vendors and markets—this will save money and enhance your culinary experience.
  • Group Tours: If you’re planning day trips, consider joining group tours, which often come with discounts compared to solo travel.
