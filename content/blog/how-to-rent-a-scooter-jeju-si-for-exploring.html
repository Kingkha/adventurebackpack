Essential Requirements for Scooter Rental in Jeju-si

Before you even dream of cruising along Jeju-si’s scenic routes, understanding the crucial requirements for scooter rental is paramount. The absolute must-have is an International Driving Permit (IDP) valid for motorcycles, presented alongside your original domestic driver’s license and passport. Without a valid IDP, reputable rental shops will not rent to you, no exceptions. Typically, renters must be at least 21 years old. I always advise double-checking your IDP’s validity well before your trip, as Korean regulations are quite stringent. Be prepared to provide a security deposit, usually via credit card, which covers potential damages or fines during your Jeju Island transport.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Do I need an International Driving Permit to rent a scooter in Jeju-si?

A: Yes, an International Driving Permit (IDP) valid for motorcycles is mandatory. You must present it along with your original domestic driver’s license and passport. Without an IDP, rental shops will not allow you to rent a scooter in Jeju-si, adhering strictly to South Korean regulations.

Q: How much does it typically cost to rent a scooter in Jeju-si?

A: Scooter rental prices in Jeju-si generally range from 30,000 to 60,000 KRW per day, depending on the scooter model, rental duration, and insurance coverage. Longer rental periods often offer discounted daily rates. Always factor in a security deposit, which is usually refunded upon safe return.

Q: Is it safe to drive a scooter in Jeju-si?

A: Driving a scooter in Jeju-si can be safe if you ride cautiously and adhere to local traffic laws. Roads are generally well-maintained, but be aware of traffic, especially in urban areas, and local driving habits. Always wear a helmet, drive defensively, and choose a well-maintained scooter from a reputable rental company.
