Alicante Highlights

Alicante is a vibrant coastal city located in Spain that beautifully combines history, culture, and stunning landscapes to offer a plethora of unforgettable experiences. Known for its picturesque beaches, mouthwatering cuisine, and rich cultural heritage, Alicante has something delightful for every kind of traveler. In this article, we will guide you through the top 10 Alicante highlights that will help you make the most of your visit, showcasing the essential attractions, local delights, and unique experiences. Whether you’re a beach lover, a foodie, or a history buff, Alicante is sure to enchant you with its charm.

1. Explore the Historic Santa Barbara Castle in Alicante

The Santa Barbara Castle is undoubtedly one of the main Alicante highlights. Perched on Mount Benacantil, this historic fortress offers breathtaking views of the city and the Mediterranean Sea. As you ascend, you will encounter intriguing architecture and learn about the castle’s history, which dates back to the 9th century.

Additionally, the castle is accessible by foot, or you can opt for a panoramic lift that adds to the experience. While exploring, make sure to visit the various defensive towers and admire the stunning vistas. As a pro tip, visit during sunset for a truly magical atmosphere and perfect photo opportunities.

2. Relax at the Beautiful Postiguet Beach in Alicante

Postiguet Beach is a must-visit destination for sun seekers and beach lovers. Located just a short walk from the city center, this urban beach boasts golden sands and crystal-clear waters. It is the perfect spot to unwind and soak up the sun after a day of exploration.

Moreover, Postiguet Beach offers various water sports and beach umbrellas for rent, ensuring a fun-filled day. You can also find a number of beachfront bars and restaurants where you can indulge in refreshing cocktails or tapas. Therefore, bringing a beach towel and sunscreen is essential for a comfortable experience at this stunning beach.

3. Discover Local Cuisine at Alicante’s Mercado Central

Alicante’s Mercado Central is the ultimate place to experience local cuisine and Culture. This bustling market offers a vibrant atmosphere filled with fresh produce, local meats, seafood, and artisan goods. Here, you can let your senses guide you as you explore the colorful stalls.

Notably, this market is an excellent spot to sample tapas and local delicacies. You can also purchase ingredients to cook at home or simply enjoy a meal amidst the friendly locals. If you’re a food lover, don’t forget to ask vendors for recommendations to fully enjoy the flavors of Alicante.

4. Stroll Along the Explanada de España: A Scenic Promenade

The Explanada de España is one of the most picturesque spots in Alicante, and a must-visit for every traveler. This iconic promenade stretches along the coast, lined with palm trees and vibrant tiles that create a stunning mosaic. As you stroll along the walkway, you can breathe in the fresh sea air and admire the beautiful Mediterranean views.

Moreover, there are various benches where you can sit and relax while people-watching or enjoying street performances. Additionally, the promenade is perfect for enjoying a leisurely evening walk as the sun sets, illuminating the sky with hues of orange and pink. Transitioning from day to night, the Explanada comes alive with various food vendors and musicians. This is a fantastic opportunity to absorb the local Culture!

5. Immerse Yourself in the Art at Museo de Arte Contemporáneo

A visit to the Museo de Arte Contemporáneo is essential for art lovers exploring Alicante. This contemporary art museum features an impressive collection of both Spanish and international works, showcasing various artistic styles and mediums. Importantly, the museum houses exhibitions that change regularly, ensuring there is always something new to discover.

Not only can you admire the innovative artworks, but you can also participate in workshops and guided tours. Consequently, this museum offers a unique opportunity to learn about contemporary art directly from knowledgeable curators. Perhaps one of the most striking aspects is the museum’s architecture, which beautifully contrasts the surrounding urban landscape.

6. Experience the Nightlife in Alicante’s Old Town

The Nightlife in Alicante’s Old Town is an exhilarating experience for travelers looking to enjoy lively entertainment. During the evenings, the narrow, cobbled streets come alive with bustling bars and vibrant nightclubs. Each venue has its own unique charm, drawn by both locals and visitors alike. As a result, you will find a variety of music, ranging from traditional Spanish guitar to popular DJ sets.

Furthermore, many outdoor terraces provide a scenic backdrop for an evening drink under the stars. Whether you prefer sipping a cocktail at a chic bar or mingling with friends over local wines, you’ll find numerous options. Additionally, be sure to try the local delicacies that some bars offer as you soak up the vivacious atmosphere. Indeed, experiencing Alicante’s Nightlife is an adventure you won’t want to miss!

7. Visit the Colorful San Juan Beach in Alicante

San Juan Beach is undoubtedly one of the top highlights in Alicante. Renowned for its stunning coastline and vibrant atmosphere, it stretches for over 3 kilometers, making it perfect for sunseekers and families alike. When you arrive, you’ll immediately notice the golden sands and crystal-clear waters inviting you for a refreshing dip.

Not only is the beach great for swimming, but it also offers a variety of water sports. For instance, you can try windsurfing or paddleboarding. Many visitors find that participating in these Activities adds excitement to their day. Moreover, San Juan Beach is lined with beach bars and restaurants, which provide delicious local cuisine and refreshing drinks. Therefore, spending a whole day here, lounging and sampling delicious bites, is highly recommended.

8. Enjoy Traditional Tapas Tasting in Alicante’s Bars

No visit to Alicante is complete without indulging in its traditional tapas. The city boasts a vibrant culinary scene, where tapas play a central role. As you explore the charming streets, you’ll find numerous bars offering a feast for your senses. For example, head over to the historic Old Town, where you can taste authentic dishes while soaking up the local atmosphere.

Make sure to try typical tapas such as patatas bravas, gambas al ajillo (garlic shrimp), and jamón ibérico. Each dish tells a story of the region’s rich Culture. Furthermore, pairing your tapas with a glass of local wine or horchata elevates the experience. Hence, wandering from bar to bar for a tapas tasting adventure is both delightful and satisfying.

9. Join the Festivities of the Bonfires of San Juan

The Bonfires of San Juan is a spectacular festival held annually in Alicante, usually starting on June 20th and culminating on June 24th. This event celebrates the summer solstice and showcases the city’s vibrant spirit. Visitors who join in on the festivities will be treated to an unforgettable experience filled with fireworks, music, and spectacular bonfires.

During the festival, large sculptures made of wood and cardboard are built and displayed throughout the city. As the night falls, these creations are set ablaze, creating a magical atmosphere. Additionally, traditional foods and drinks are widely available, allowing you to taste regional specialties while celebrating with locals and fellow travelers. Thus, participating in the Bonfires of San Juan gives you a unique insight into Alicante’s cultural heritage and traditions.

10. Take a Day Trip to the Beautiful Island of Tabarca

When visiting Alicante, one of the top highlights is undoubtedly a day trip to the enchanting Island of Tabarca. This small island, located just off the coast, is the perfect getaway for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of the mainland. To get there, you can catch a ferry from the Alicante harbor, which offers stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea during the journey.

Upon arrival, you’ll be greeted by crystal-clear waters and picturesque landscapes. The island is known for its charming streets, historic architecture, and beautiful beaches, making it a fantastic destination for both relaxation and exploration. Moreover, you can explore the old town, which features remnants of its past, including a 17th-century fort and a lovely church.

After a day of swimming and sunbathing, savor local seafood dishes at one of the island’s restaurants. Tabarca is famous for its fresh fish, and dining here will undoubtedly be a highlight of your trip. So, if you’re looking to experience the beauty of Alicante’s coastline, a trip to Tabarca is a must!

Alicante is a city rich in culture, history, and natural beauty, making it a perfect destination for travelers seeking authentic experiences.
