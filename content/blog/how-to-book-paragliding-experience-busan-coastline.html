Essential Safety Tips for Your Busan Paragliding Flight

Safety should always be your top priority when planning a paragliding experience. For tandem flights, you’ll be securely harnessed with a certified instructor, who handles all the technical aspects. Ensure your chosen operator is licensed and that their instructors have extensive experience. Before your flight, you’ll receive a detailed briefing covering safety protocols, takeoff, and landing procedures. Always listen carefully and follow their instructions. When to Go & What to Expect: Seasonal Insights

The best times for paragliding in Busan are generally spring (April-May) and autumn (September-October) when the weather is mild, skies are clear, and winds are stable. Summer can be good too, but watch out for heat and sudden thunderstorms. Winter flights are possible but can be quite cold. Wind conditions are paramount; flights are weather-dependent, so be prepared for potential rescheduling. Your instructor will advise on the day. Tandem paragliding flights typically last 10-20 minutes, depending on the thermals, but the memory lasts a lifetime! From personal observation, early mornings or late afternoons often offer the calmest conditions and most beautiful light for photos. Keep an eye on local Busan events that might coincide with your trip, but remember that paragliding requires specific weather.
