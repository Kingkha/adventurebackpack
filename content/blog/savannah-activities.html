Savannah Activities

Savannah, Georgia, is a city brimming with charm and history, offering countless activities for every type of traveler. From its stunning parks and historic sites to its vibrant culinary scene and spooky ghost tours, there’s no shortage of things to do in this enchanting Southern city. In this article, we will highlight the top 10 Savannah activities that showcase the unique culture and spirit of the region. Whether you’re a history buff, a food lover, or just seeking adventure, Savannah has something engaging for you.

1. Exploring the Historic District: A Stroll Through Time

The Historic District of Savannah is a treasure trove waiting to be explored. This area is famous for its stunning architecture, cobblestone streets, and lush greenery. When you wander through the district, you will encounter numerous squares, each with its unique story. For instance, Chippewa Square is not only picturesque but also known for its iconic role in the film “Forrest Gump.”

Moreover, many of the buildings date back to the 18th and 19th centuries, offering a glimpse into the city’s rich past. Walking tours are available, providing you with insights into Savannah’s history, culture, and local anecdotes. Additionally, don’t forget your camera; the sunsets here are breathtaking! As you take this historical journey, remember to stop by the local boutiques and cafés.

2. Enjoying a Ghost Tour: Experience Savannah’s Spooky Side

For thrill-seekers, one of the must-try Savannah activities is the ghost tour. Savannah is often dubbed as one of the most haunted cities in America. A ghost tour typically takes you through some of the oldest parts of the city, immersing you in spine-chilling tales and folklore.

Many of these tours begin in the Historic District, making it convenient. You might visit spots like the Rogers House or the eerie old prison. While some tales are spine-tingling, others are interlaced with local history, enhancing your experience. After all, what better way to learn about Savannah’s haunting past? Don’t forget to keep an open mind as you explore the shadows of this enchanting city!

3. Visiting Forsyth Park: Nature’s Oasis in the City

Forsyth Park is a stunning 30-acre oasis right in the heart of Savannah. This lush park is perfect for anyone seeking a break from the urban hustle. The park features beautiful walking paths, landscaped gardens, and the iconic Forsyth Fountain, which is a favorite for photographs.

In addition, you can enjoy various recreational activities. Whether it’s jogging, picnicking, or simply relaxing on a bench, Forsyth Park has something for everyone. The park often hosts cultural events and local art exhibitions, adding to its vibrancy. Therefore, it’s not just a park; it’s a gathering place for the community. Overall, visiting Forsyth Park should definitely be on your itinerary when exploring Savannah activities.

4. Discovering River Street: Shopping and Dining by the Water

River Street is truly a vibrant hub in Savannah, where the historic charm meets modern-day excitement. While strolling along this waterfront area, you will find a delightful mix of boutiques, art galleries, and restaurants. One of the Highlights is the unique shops that offer local handmade goods, which are perfect for souvenirs. Moreover, as you walk, the views of the Savannah River will captivate your senses.

Additionally, the dining options are diverse, ranging from classic Southern cuisine to fresh seafood. For a truly local experience, try River House Seafood, renowned for its fresh catches and river views. Not only is the food exceptional, but the ambiance is perfect for a relaxing meal.

Remember to catch the sunset at River Street; it’s an unforgettable experience!

5. Taking a Savannah Riverboat Cruise: Scenic Views and Southern Charm

A Savannah Riverboat Cruise offers an exciting way to explore the beautiful scenery along the river. As you board, you’ll be welcomed by Southern hospitality that defines Savannah. The cruises typically feature live music, delicious dining options, and, of course, stunning views of the city’s skyline.

Throughout your journey, you’ll learn about the history of Savannah from informative narrations. Many visitors opt for the sunset cruise to enjoy breathtaking views as the sun dips below the horizon. Furthermore, there’s something magical about gliding along the river while soaking in the sights and sounds of this enchanting city.

6. Exploring the Bonaventure Cemetery: A Step into Southern History

Stepping into the Bonaventure Cemetery feels like walking through history. This beautiful cemetery, famous for its stunning live oaks and moss-covered tombstones, is a must-visit for anyone interested in Savannah’s unique past. As you wander the paths, you’ll encounter notable figures and artists from Savannah’s history, each with their own story.

While exploring, take time to appreciate the amazing sculptures and monuments. The serene atmosphere makes it an ideal spot for reflection and contemplation. Although some may find cemeteries eerie, Bonaventure offers a peaceful retreat set amidst nature.

Pro tip: Visit during the early morning for a quieter, more atmospheric experience.

7. Tasting Southern Cuisine: A Culinary Journey Through Savannah

When it comes to Savannah activities, indulging in the local Southern cuisine is an absolute must. The city boasts a vibrant food scene that blends traditional flavors with modern twists. One cannot visit Savannah without trying classic dishes such as shrimp and grits, fried green tomatoes, and the unique peach cobbler. In fact, many restaurants pride themselves on using locally sourced ingredients, which not only supports the community but also enhances the overall dining experience.

Moreover, exploring the culinary landscape of Savannah can be quite the adventure. Consider joining a food tour to sample various eateries, where you’ll meet passionate chefs who share their stories and the history behind their dishes. As a pro tip, don’t forget to pair your meal with a selection of local craft beers or sweet tea to complete your meal.

8. Participating in the Savannah Music Fest: A Melodic Experience

Another incredible aspect of Savannah activities is the annual Savannah Music Fest. This event showcases a diverse lineup of musical genres, ranging from jazz to bluegrass. Typically held in the spring, this festival attracts visitors from around the country, eager to experience live performances by renowned artists as well as local talents. As the festival takes over various venues throughout the city, you can immerse yourself in a vibrant atmosphere filled with excitement and energy.

Additionally, attending the Savannah Music Fest is a fantastic way to connect with the community. You will find that many attendees share stories about their favorite performances, creating a memorable experience that transcends music. Therefore, if you’re a music lover, make sure to include this festival in your travel itinerary!

9. Visiting the Savannah History Museum: Learning About the Past

For those seeking to delve deeper into the city’s rich history, visiting the Savannah History Museum is a top choice among Savannah activities. Located in the heart of the Historic District, this museum showcases fascinating exhibits that chronicle Savannah’s past, including its role in the American Revolutionary War and the Civil War. As you wander through the museum, you’ll encounter numerous artifacts, photographs, and documents that bring historical events to life.

Moreover, the museum offers guided tours, which can enhance your visit by providing in-depth knowledge shared by passionate guides. This interactive experience allows visitors to learn and engage with Savannah’s history on a more personal level. In summary, a trip to the Savannah History Museum is not only educational but also an enjoyable way to appreciate the city’s heritage.

10. Relaxing at Tybee Island: The Beach Experience Just a Short Trip Away

When you think of Savannah activities, don’t overlook the nearby gem of Tybee Island. Just a short drive from the historic city, Tybee Island offers stunning beaches, a laid-back atmosphere, and a plethora of outdoor activities. This picturesque destination is perfect for anyone looking to unwind and soak up the sun.

As you step onto Tybee Island, the soft sands and gentle ocean waves wrap around you. For instance, the popular Main Beach is ideal for sunbathing, swimming, or simply relaxing with a good book. Furthermore, Tybee’s coastline is dotted with unique beachfront restaurants where you can indulge in fresh seafood while enjoying breathtaking views.

Additionally, don’t miss out on exploring the Tybee Island Lighthouse. Climbing to the top provides an incredible view of the surrounding area.

Tip: Visit the lighthouse early in the morning to avoid crowds and fully enjoy the tranquility of this beautiful spot.

Overall, Tybee Island is the perfect escape for those looking to add some beach bliss to their Savannah itinerary.

Savannah’s unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor activities offers an array of unforgettable experiences for every traveler. Whether you’re wandering through the historic district, indulging in Southern cuisine, or taking in the views from a riverboat, the activities in Savannah promise to leave lasting memories.
