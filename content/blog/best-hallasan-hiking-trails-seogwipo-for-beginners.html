Your First Steps: The Eoseungsaengak Trail

For an ideal introduction to Hallasan National Park, look no further than the Eoseungsaengak Trail. This is hands down one of the best Hallasan hiking trails Seogwipo for beginners, offering a relatively short and manageable ascent to Eoseungsaengak Peak, providing stunning panoramic views of Jeju Island’s northern landscape and even the distant summit on a clear day. The entire round trip typically takes about an hour to an hour and a half, making it perfect for a leisurely morning or afternoon outing. More Scenic, Still Simple: Yeongsil & Eorimok Trails

While not leading to the summit, the Yeongsil and Eorimok trails offer spectacular, less strenuous alternatives that are fantastic for beginners ready for a bit more mileage. The Yeongsil Course is particularly renowned for its dramatic rock formations, often referred to as the ‘Five Hundred Generals’, and breathtaking alpine views. Eorimok, on the other hand, provides a more forested and tranquil experience, gradually ascending through beautiful woodland before opening up to incredible vistas. Both trails are incredibly well-maintained, ensuring a safe and pleasant journey. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Do I need a permit or reservation for beginner Hallasan trails?

A: Generally, no permits or reservations are required for the Eoseungsaengak, Yeongsil, or Eorimok trails on Hallasan. These routes are accessible to the public during operating hours. However, always check the Hallasan National Park official website before your visit for any updated regulations, especially concerning access during specific seasons or adverse weather conditions.

Q: What’s the best time of year for beginner Hallasan hikes?

A: Spring (April-May) and autumn (October-November) are often considered the best times for beginner hikes on Hallasan. Spring brings blooming azaleas and comfortable temperatures, while autumn offers stunning fall foliage. Summer can be hot and humid with potential typhoons, and winter brings snow and ice, requiring specialized gear and more challenging conditions for novices.

Q: Are there facilities like restrooms or shops on the beginner trails?

A: Yes, trailheads like Eoseungsaengak, Yeongsil, and Eorimok generally have visitor centers with restrooms, small shops, or vending machines for drinks and snacks. However, facilities are limited once you are on the actual trail. It’s best to use facilities at the trailhead and carry enough water and snacks for your hike, as well as pack out any trash.
